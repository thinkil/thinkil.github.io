About Us
Think Illinois is a student organization that allows students of all backgrounds to brainstorm, create, and foster new ideas and innovations. At bi-weekly meetings, students explore different topics and solve problems within these fields. To accomplish this, members break off into small, academically diverse groups in order to generate and discuss solutions. Meetings conclude when small groups rejoin to present their ideas. Members are encouraged to pursue these concepts and designs outside of meetings. In addition to general meetings, Think Illinois hosts student mini-lectures, discussions, workshops, and social events.
FAQ
So what does Think Illinois do?
Basically, we get together as a group regularly and discuss problems and solutions, focusing on creativity and brainstorming.
Why was Think Illinois formed?
At University of Illinois, we noticed a lot of great groups and organizations that focused on creating products from ideas, but no groups that looked at the creation of ideas themselves. We formed Think Illinois to fill this gap that we saw.
Are there any leadership opportunities in Think Illinois?
Yes! Anyone in the group can volunteer to lead meetings or discussions, and there are also opportunities to become involved in the Think Illinois executive board.
How can I get involved?
Just come to one of our meetings or discussions! It's as easy as that.
How do you choose a topic?
Topics are chosen and voted on by members at each of our meetings.
How often are meetings?
General meetings are held every two weeks while classes are in session.
Will there be food?
Definitely.
What should I do to prepare for a meeting?
On this website (at the members portal), there will be links to articles related to the topic that will be discussed at the next meeting. Feel free to look over these, though it isn't strictly required.
When is your next meeting?
Our events page should tell you.
Any other questions?
Let us know! Email us at thinkillinois@gmail.com or reach out to us on Facebook or Twitter.
